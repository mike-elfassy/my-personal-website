Intro to Birdwatching - October 15th, 2022

Thanks for joining the bird walk today, together we counted 47 species.

We couldn't have asked for better weather; it was warm in the sun yet cool in the shade and that's what fall is all about. The birds appreciated it too and we had solid avian highlights: a Belted Kingfisher rattling over lullwater, close looks at a Cape May Warbler, and visible ruby on a Ruby-crowned Kinglet. Better yet was seeing all the raptor activity, especially the moment when two Ravens, a Peregrine Falcon, and A Red-tailed Hawk all circled over lookout hill. At one point, it seemed like the peregrine nipped at the raven's tail feathers. How cool.

Intro to Birdwatching - May 21st, 2022

Thanks for joining the bird walk yesterday, the north walk (pools and ravine) counted 25 species, and the south walk (lullwater and wellhouse) counted 42!

For the south walk, Blackpolls, Eastern Kingbirds, and Baltimore Orioles were heard and seen just about everywhere. Nesting birds were abundant as well and it was sweet to watch Robins and Orioles feeding their nestlings! Lastly, two birds gave us a ID challenge: two infrequent birds: an Olive-sided Flycatcher and a molting juvenile Broad-winged Hawk (hard!), both seen by the Monument.

Intro to Birdwatching - April 30th, 2022

Thanks for joining the bird walk today, together we counted 31 species.

We had amazing views of a Green Heron by the lower pool and it was especially cool to watch it hunt and see its crest. In the Ravine, we had other notable sightings: a pair of Tufted Titmice%u2014usually a common winter bird though notably absent this year%u2014a perching female Eastern Towhee, and close looks at a Black-and-white Warbler in the Ravine.
